## Overview

The Alfa Laval MAB 103 Centrifuge is a solid-retaining (aka solid bowl; manual-clean), [disc-stack centrifuge](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/disc-stack-centrifuge/) ideal for the continuous, three-phase separation of fuels, such as [diesel](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/diesel-centrifuge/), [biodiesel](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/biodiesel-centrifuge/), and turbine lube oil.

With a 1 HP Motor, the MAB 103 centrifuge has a bowl RPM of 8,600 and can process 3 GPM of Diesel Fuel or 1 GPM of Turbine Lube Oil.

## Applications

The Alfa Laval MAB-103 is a purifier-type disc-stack centrifuge. It is designed to separate water (moisture) and fine sediment from mineral oil and lubricating oil. It is primarily used for purifying fuels such as [diesel](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/diesel-fuel-purifier/), kerosene, etc.

The MAB-103 is also used for separating water and fine sediment from [lubricating oil](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/lube-oil-centrifuge/), [hydraulic oil](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/hydraulic-oil-centrifuge/), and other mineral oils.

## Operation

The fuel or mineral oil is fed into the rotating bowl of the MAB-103 centrifuge. The bowl exerts a high g-force (8,500 Gs) on the process fluid. The centrifugal force causes rapid sedimentation of the solid particles down to 0.5 Microns.

This separated sludge should be manually evacuated by the operator. The heavy phase (water) phase also settles out and exits the bowl through a separate outlet.

### Operation

The process fluid is fed to the rotating centrifuge bowl from the top (1) and is sped up in the distributor (2). It then enters the disc stack (3), and it is between the discs that the separation occurs.

![Alfa-Laval-WSB-203-Centrifuge-Bowl-251x300](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20251%20300'%3E%3C/svg%3E)

Alfa-Laval-WSB-203-Centrifuge-Bowl

The high centrifugal force pushes the water-based fluid and heavy solids to the bowl wall. The solids accumulate here, and the water-based fluid travels over the top disc (4) and exits the bowl through the open outlet (5). The oil (light phase) is moved towards the bowl center and exits the bowl through the open outlet (6).

The centrifuge needs to be periodically stopped for manual removal of separated solids.

The rotating bowl assembly is mounted on a vertical 'bowl spindle' (7). A horizontally mounted motor rotates the spindle via a worm gear.
