## Minor Service and Maintenance

Periodic maintenance of the centrifuge bowl goes a long way toward the reliability and optimum performance of the centrifuge. This servicing is further divided into bowl service and centrifuge maintenance.

### Minor Bowl Service Interval

The specific centrifuge application defines the service interval of this minor bowl service. In other words, processing benign fluids such as lubricating oil could have a period of 3-6 months between minor bowl service.

The processing of aggressive chemicals or fluids with abrasive solids requires the bowl service to be performed every 2-3 weeks or sooner.

We recommend the centrifuge user frequently inspect the centrifuge during the initial startup phase to determine the condition of the bowl seals. The user can decide the period between bowl servicing based on the bowl condition during these inspections.

### Centrifuge Bowl Service (Re-Gasketting)

During the regular operation of the centrifuge, the seals and gaskets in the bowl are exposed to varying temperatures, process fluid, and sludge deposits. Over time this exposure deteriorates the seals and affects centrifuge performance.

A periodic bowl service that includes replacing all seals and gaskets is essential to maintain centrifuge performance.

A bowl service involves changing the o-rings, oil seals, gaskets, and other consumable parts. The gaskets are lubricated with an exceptionally long-lasting lubricant specific for centrifuge seals. The operator must thoroughly clean the seal grooves and other crevices to remove accumulated sludge and other debris.

### Centrifuge Bowl Maintenance

Cleaning the [operating water system](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/disc-stack-centrifuge-operating-water/) under the bowl is essential to this periodic minor service. The primary purpose of cleaning this part of the system is to clean out sediment and mineral deposits that can prevent the operation of the sludge ejection mechanism.

A simple cleaning with vinegar or any mineral cleaning solvent is sufficient. Replacing o-rings with the proper lubrication is also essential to the cleaning process.

Any nozzles in the operating water system must be thoroughly cleaned and blown out with compressed air.

## Major Service

A primary service of the centrifuge involves all the aspects of the minor service above. In addition to the consumable parts, this service includes replacing mechanical parts of the centrifuge transmission, including bearings, friction pads, springs, etc.

### Service Interval of Major Centrifuge Service

Industrial centrifuge manufacturers recommend a **major overhaul of the disc centrifuge every 2-3 years.** This period between the service is based on the expected lifespan of the components. The expected wear of specific components in the bowl also defines this period.

An unexpected failure of a transmission component could require a major centrifuge rebuild. For example, a sudden bearing failure could make the centrifuge inoperable. A major service is unavoidable under such circumstances.

### Bowl Cleaning

A thorough cleaning of the stack bowl as part of the major service process involves cleaning the disc stack and other components of the liquid passage. The distributor, cone, and the upper chamber of the top disc are all areas that can accumulate sludge during operation.

The sediment can scratch the individual disc surface that the sludge particles stick to.

An industrial parts washer or other mechanical cleaning equipment is well suited for cleaning these components. Soaking the bowl discs in a mild detergent or special disc cleaner liquid removes the sludge buildup. Subsequent rinsing of the disc stack follows the soaking process.

![Electropolished Bowl Discs](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20300%20368'%3E%3C/svg%3E)

**Electropolished Stack Discs**

As an added step, a worn disc stack may be electropolished to smoothen the disc surface and resist the possibility of sludge particles sticking to the discs.

### Transmission Rebuild

A major service of the disc centrifuge includes a transmission rebuild and the bowl cleaning mentioned above. This part of the service involves the replacement of all bearings and related parts. In a centrifuge with a friction clutch, replacing friction pads is also a part of the service. These parts replacements reduce the possibility of unexpected centrifuge breakdown during operations.

The inspection of the gears in the gear train is a crucial part of this service. Uneven wear of the gear teeth may require the gears to be changed. Changing the transmission gears as a complete set, not as individual gears, is essential. The image below shows a typical disc-centrifuge gear set.

[![Disc Centrifuge Gear Set](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20600%20300'%3E%3C/svg%3E)](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/Centrifuge-Gear-600-1.jpg)

**Disc Centrifuge Gear Set**

If the centrifuge was experiencing high vibration levels before service, it requires replacing the vibration-dampening spring in the spring casing.

While draining the gearbox oil, the operator should look for metal shavings in the oil. The presence of brass or steel metal particles in the oil implies excessive wear of the gear set. These metal particles damage the bearings through the splash lubrication. Therefore, replacing all bearings and thoroughly cleaning the gearbox is essential.

## Centrifuge Rebuild

An old disc centrifuge that has been out of service for a prolonged period requires a complete overhaul or rebuild. Often the centrifuge is of unknown origin without any information regarding its previous use.

In such cases, the centrifuge's complete and thorough rebuild is crucial for safe future operation.

Our centrifuge rebuild process is detailed and involves the following steps.

* Strip down your old centrifuge to bare metal.
* Clean all bowl and transmission parts.
* Sandblast and clean frame parts.
* Prime and paint frame with durable epoxy topcoat.
* Inspect and test all critical parts for dimensional tolerance and function.
* Replace or repair worn or questionable parts with new or rebuilt components.
* Replace all bearings with OEM recommended bearings. These are special bearings made for high-speed industrial centrifuges.
* Replace all consumable parts, i.e., O-Rings, Gaskets, Friction Material, Belts, etc.
* Rebuild centrifuge to original specifications.
* Test centrifuge for all mechanical parameters and operational functions.

### Rebuilt Centrifuge Testing

Testing the rebuilt centrifuge is essential to verify the machine’s functionality and overall mechanical status. The functionality refers to the fluid flow through the centrifuge and the operation of the self-cleaning bowl.

Leakproof operation of the inlet and outlet fluid pathways is the first step if the centrifuge system has external piping, pressure testing of the pipes is a crucial step in the process.

The bowl opening and closing during the sludge ejection process is the second step of the testing process. This step involves monitoring the operating water pressure and flow to determine the manufacturer’s specifications parameters.

The testing could be with water or mineral oil, depending on the centrifuge application.

Monitoring the drive motor current draw indicates the health of the centrifuge gear train.

The cost of repairing an industrial centrifuge depends on the type of centrifuge and the extent of wear or damage that needs to be fixed.

## Parts Repairing for Wear Damage

Processing fluids with abrasive or metal particles has a detrimental effect on specific bowl components. The sliding piston is especially subject to wear and tear due to the separated solids' continuous contact during the sludge ejection process.

The image below shows the typical damage seen on the sliding piston.

![Alfa Laval Centrifuge Sliding Piston Erosion](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20400%20223'%3E%3C/svg%3E)

**Alfa Laval Centrifuge Sliding Piston Erosion**

Minimal wear on the piston is fixable by local sanding of high spots.

Local pitting damage on parts exposed to operating water is often remedied by smoothening the surface with soft grinding. Excessive pitting beyond 0.2 mm requires the replacement of the affected parts.
