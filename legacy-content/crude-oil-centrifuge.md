## Design and Operation

An [industrial centrifuge](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/industrial-centrifuge/) designed to dewater crude oil and separate particulate matter is called a crude oil centrifuge. These centrifuges exert high centrifugal forces (over 8,000 Gs) to continuously remove all water and sludge particles. Sale oil with less than 0.1% BS&W can be reliably reclaimed.

Crude oil centrifuges are mechanical separating machines used in O&G production applications. They perform a variety of separating functions in petroleum production. From drilling mud recovery to oil washing, centrifuges are versatile tools in the petroleum industry.

![Alfa Laval 3-Phase Centrifuge Class 1 Div 2 Crude Oil Hawaii](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/Alfa-Laval-3-Phase-Centrifuge-Class-1-Div-2-Crude-Oil-Hawaii-600.jpg)

Heavy sludge is the primary contaminant in all types of crude oil-related streams. It must be removed to protect downstream equipment such as pumps, separators, wellheads, etc.

A decanter centrifuge is the first line of defense for these heavy, abrasive solids. Alfa Laval [three-phase decanters](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/three-phase-decanter/) efficiently remove sludge and produce sludge-free oil. De-sludged oil is much easier to handle and treat for recovery of sale oil.

In general, these oilfield centrifuges are subject to considerable wear and abrasion. Therefore, these oil centrifuges need to have robust construction and wear protection.

## Applications in Oil and Gas Production

### Drilling Mud Recovery

Solids control centrifuges are also known as drilling mud centrifuges or decanters. Oil well drilling requires lubrication for efficient operation. This lubricant is a mix of water and barite, which is a mineral.

Barite allows the drilling lubricant to work under high pressure in deep wells. The drilling mud carries crushed rocks when it flows back to the surface. Centrifuges separate drill fluids from the crushed solids as the drilling mud returns to the surface.

Drilling mud solids are heavy and abrasive. They need heavy-duty gearboxes, wear protection, tiled conveyors, and precision speed control. Wear protection ensures the highest efficiency and durability.

### Oil-Based Drilling Mud Recovery

Oil-based drilling mud (OBM) often uses diesel as the lubricant. The oil enhances the lubricity and better temperature resistance without breaking down.

Recovery of diesel from OBM is desirable due to the cost of oil. The diesel in used OBM carries colloidal solids, and separating these suspended solids is essential before reusing the oil.

Decanters do not exert centrifugal force high enough to extract these suspended solids. A high-speed disc stack centrifuge generates enough force to separate these small particles.

Dolphin Centrifuge's oil cleaning system is suitable for recovering diesel from de-sludged OBM. Our solution uses a self-cleaning disc stack centrifuge to extract the colloidal solids. It can also break emulsified oil depending on the type of emulsion.

### Tank Bottoms Recovery

Crude oil extracted from wells carries saltwater and sand or other sediments. Producers use settling tanks to skim sale oil from the top of settling tanks. The residue left in the settling tanks is known as tank bottoms.

Tank bottoms contain Basic Sediment & Water (BS&W). Skimming of sale oil leaves plenty of good oil in tank bottoms. This oil can be recoverable using our centrifuge system for extra revenue.

An oilfield centrifuge is a cost-effective solution for [tank bottom processing](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/crude-oil-tank-bottom-recovery-centrifuge/) to recover sale oil. The centrifuge also reduces tank bottom sludge, thus significantly reducing tank cleaning costs.

Salt Water Disposals (SWDs) use Alfa Laval three-phase centrifuges for dewatering crude oil from produced water.

### Refinery Slop Oil

Oil refinery waste streams are also known as refinery slop. This waste stream consists of wastewater, crude oil, and sludge. It often contains varying amounts of oil emulsions as well.

A refinery slop treatment system includes a crude oil decanter for de-sludging. A high-speed disc stack centrifuge separates the liquid phases in the de-sludged oil. Using chemicals helps the centrifuge break the oily-water mix if the emulsion is present.

Therefore, both the decanter and disc stack centrifuge are well suited for refinery slop oil treatment.

### Water Washing

Heavy metals, such as lead, iron, arsenic, nickel, etc., are present in crude oil. Though their presence in trace levels is not an issue, higher levels of metals can cause a problem for the producer.

Oil companies do not want to buy crude oil with heavy metals beyond their specified level.

Certain types of crude oil are washable with water to remove heavy metals by adsorption. Water absorbs the metals and needs to be separated. A 3-phase disc stack centrifuge recovers the crude oil from the wash water.

Of course, this process creates a stream of water with adsorbed heavy metals. This contaminated water stream needs disposal through proper means.

## Advantages

Centrifuges offer several benefits over conventional heating and settling of crude oil to separate BS&W. The following is a list of the main advantages of crude oil centrifuges.

* **High Throughput:** Centrifuges exert thousands of times the force of gravity, thereby causing instant separation of crude oil from the bottom sediment and water. Traditional settling works under a single force of gravity and takes much longer.
* **Compact Size:** Settling tanks have large footprints and therefore require considerable real estate for deployment. Centrifuges are comparatively much smaller to handle the same volume of oil.
* **Energy Efficient:** Centrifuges process the crude oil continuously. Therefore, they can use inline heating, which heats up only when the oil is separated. Heater treaters heat thousands of barrels of oil which is colossal energy waste.
* **Quick Deployment:** Compared to tank farms with related permitting and land acquisition, the centrifuge can be deployed in a fraction of the time.
* **Less Capital Expense:** Considering the cost of land, tankage, permits, and disposal facilities, the cost of heating and settling is far greater than that of a centrifuge system.
* **Reduced OIl Losses:** It is possible to produce oil with less than 1% BS%W with a centrifuge system with minimal losses. A considerable amount of oil is lost in drained bottoms to achieve similar quality oil.
* **Better Quality Oil:** The high g-force exerted by centrifuges leads to a much higher quality of crude oil when compared to oil produced by simple gravity settling.
* **Reduced Disposal Costs:** A centrifuge-produced dry sludge cake (reduced disposal volume) reduces disposal costs. Conventional tank bottoms contain higher amounts of oil and water, leading to larger disposal volumes.
