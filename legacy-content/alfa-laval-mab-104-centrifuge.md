## Overview

The Alfa Laval MAB 104 Centrifuge is a solid-retaining (aka solid bowl; manual-clean), disc-stack centrifuge ideal for the continuous, three-phase separation of fuels, such as [diesel](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/diesel-centrifuge/), [biodiesel](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/biodiesel-centrifuge/), and [turbine lube oil](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/lube-oil-centrifuge/).

With a 2 HP Motor, the MAB 104 centrifuge has a bowl RPM of 7,500 and can process 6 GPM of Diesel Fuel or 1 GPM of Turbine Lube Oil.

## Applications

The **Alfa Laval MAB-104** is a purifier type disc-stack centrifuge. It is designed to separate water (moisture) and fine sediment from mineral oil and lubricating oil. It is primarily used for purifying fuels such as [diesel](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/diesel-fuel-purifier/), kerosene, etc. The MAB-104 is also used for separating water and fine sediment from [lubrication oil](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/lube-oil-centrifuge/), [hydraulic oil](https://dolphincentrifuge.com/hydraulic-oil-centrifuge/), and other mineral oil.

For offshore applications, the centrifuge is configured similar to the OCM-104-Ex configuration with explosion-proof ATEX Zone II.

## Operation

The process oil is fed to the rotating centrifuge bowl from the top (1) and is sped-up in the distributor (2). It then enters the disc stack (3), and it is between the discs that the separation occurs.

![Alfa-Laval-WSB-203-Centrifuge-Bowl-251x300](data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20251%20300'%3E%3C/svg%3E)

The high centrifugal force pushes the water and heavy solids to the bowl wall. The solids accumulate here, and the water travels over the top disc (4) and exits the bowl through the open outlet (5).

The oil (light phase) is moved towards the bowl center and exits the bowl through the open outlet (6).

The centrifuge needs to be periodically stopped for manual removal of separated solids.

The rotating bowl assembly is mounted on a vertical 'bowl-spindle' (7). A horizontally mounted motor rotates the spindle via a worm-gear.
